How to Write Email Subject Lines That Get Opened

8 min read · June 2026 · ToneFixer Blog
Blog → How to Write Email Subject Lines That Get Opened

Your email subject line is the single most important factor in whether your email gets opened or ignored. Research shows that 47% of email recipients open emails based on the subject line alone. Yet most people spend just a few seconds writing theirs.

In this guide, you'll learn the proven techniques for writing subject lines that get noticed, opened, and acted on.

Why Subject Lines Matter So Much

The average person receives 120+ emails per day. In a crowded inbox, your subject line has about 2 seconds to make an impression. A great subject line creates curiosity, communicates value, and feels personal — all in under 60 characters.

7 Proven Subject Line Formulas

1. The Question

Questions create an open loop in the reader's mind that they feel compelled to close.

Example: "Are you making these 5 email mistakes?"

2. The Number

Numbers are specific and promise clear, digestible value.

Example: "3 ways to double your email open rate"

3. The Urgency

Creates a reason to open now rather than later.

Example: "Last chance: offer ends tonight"

4. The Personalization

Including the recipient's name or company increases open rates by up to 26%.

Example: "John, here's the report you requested"

5. The Curiosity Gap

Hints at something interesting without giving it all away.

Example: "The email mistake everyone makes (and how to fix it)"

6. The Direct Benefit

Clearly states what the reader will get from opening.

Example: "Save 2 hours every week with this simple trick"

7. The Follow-Up Reference

For follow-up emails, referencing your previous email works well.

Example: "Following up on my proposal from last week"

What to Avoid in Subject Lines

  • ❌ ALL CAPS — looks like spam
  • ❌ Excessive exclamation marks!!!
  • ❌ Spam trigger words: "Free," "Act now," "Limited time offer"
  • ❌ Vague subject lines: "Important" or "Hello"
  • ❌ Misleading subject lines — destroys trust

Subject Line Length

Keep subject lines under 60 characters for desktop, or under 40 characters if most of your audience reads email on mobile. Front-load the most important words in case the subject line gets cut off.
