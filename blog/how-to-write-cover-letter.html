How to Write a Cover Letter That Gets You Hired

📅 May 9, 2026 ⏱ 6 min read ✍️ ToneFixer Team

A great cover letter can be the difference between getting an interview and being ignored. While your resume tells employers what you've done, your cover letter tells them who you are and why you're the perfect fit. In this guide, you'll learn exactly how to write a cover letter that stands out — with a template you can use right away.

💡 Studies show that 83% of hiring managers say a cover letter influences their decision — even when it's not required. Always send one.

The 5 Key Parts of a Great Cover Letter

1

The Opening — Hook Them Immediately

Don't start with "I am writing to apply for..." — everyone does that. Instead, open with something that shows your enthusiasm or a specific achievement. Make the hiring manager want to keep reading in the very first sentence.

2

Why You — Show Your Value

This is the most important part. Don't just list your skills — show how your specific experience solves their specific problem. Research the company and connect what they need with what you offer.

3

Why Them — Show You Did Your Research

Mention something specific about the company — their mission, a recent achievement, or their culture. This shows you're genuinely interested, not just mass-applying to every job you see.

4

Your Best Achievement

Include one specific, measurable achievement that's relevant to the role. Numbers make you memorable. "I increased sales by 40%" is far more powerful than "I'm good at sales."

5

The Closing — Ask for the Interview

End confidently. Thank them for their time, express your enthusiasm for the role, and clearly ask for an interview. Don't be passive — be direct and professional.

Cover Letter Template

📄 Professional Cover Letter Template

[Your Name]
[Your Email] | [Your Phone] | [LinkedIn]
[Date]

[Hiring Manager's Name]
[Company Name]
[Company Address]

Dear [Hiring Manager's Name],

[Opening hook — show enthusiasm or lead with an achievement relevant to the role.]

In my [X years] of experience as a [Your Role], I have [specific achievement with numbers]. I am particularly drawn to [Company Name] because [specific reason showing you've researched them].

What I bring to this role includes:

• [Relevant skill/achievement 1]
• [Relevant skill/achievement 2]
• [Relevant skill/achievement 3]

I am excited about the opportunity to bring my expertise to [Company Name] and contribute to [specific goal or project]. I would welcome the chance to discuss how my background aligns with your needs.

Thank you for your time and consideration. I look forward to the opportunity to speak with you.

Sincerely,
[Your Name]

Common Cover Letter Mistakes to Avoid

  • Copying your resume — your cover letter should add new information, not repeat your CV
  • Making it too long — keep it to one page, 3-4 short paragraphs
  • Using generic language — customize every cover letter for the specific job
  • Focusing on what you want — focus on what you can offer the employer
  • Forgetting to proofread — one typo can cost you the interview
  • Wrong tone — too casual sounds unprofessional, too formal sounds robotic
